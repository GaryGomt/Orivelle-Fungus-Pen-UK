<h2 class="western"><strong>Understanding Nail Fungus</strong></h2>
<p>Before examining the product itself, it&rsquo;s important to understand what nail fungus is and why it can be so difficult to treat.</p>
<p>Nail fungus, medically known as&nbsp;<strong>onychomycosis</strong>, occurs when fungi infect the nail bed or the nail plate. It often begins as a small white or yellow spot under the nail tip. If left untreated, it can spread deeper, causing:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Thickened nails</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Discoloration (yellow, brown, or white)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Brittle or crumbly texture</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Distorted nail shape</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Foul odor in some cases</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Fungi thrive in warm, moist environments, making feet particularly vulnerable &mdash; especially for people who frequently wear closed shoes, visit public pools, or have sweaty feet.</p>
<p>One of the biggest challenges in treating nail fungus is the structure of the nail itself. The hard keratin layer acts as a barrier, making it difficult for topical treatments to penetrate deeply enough to reach the infection.</p>
